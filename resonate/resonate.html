RESOANTE


About the project

Resonate is an app that helps people explore and share new music playlists from around the world associated with different moods (emotions and feelings) and activities. Users can "heart" a playlist to boost it's ranking on the charts.

 The story behind the idea: We came up with the idea of Resonate while having a conversation about Bollywood movie. One of my friends, in a sarcastic tone, asked me "Why do all Bollywood movies have songs in them?”

I explained him how songs in Bollywood movies act as MacGuffin and help with the storytelling aspect of the movie. Songs in Bollywood movies help the audience to build an emphatical connection with the plot of the movie. For example, a romantic music video is played during the beginning of a new love story in order to make the audience build that passionate feeling in their minds.

This led us to the conversation about current music. We discovered a problem. Modern music is getting emotionally ambiguous day by day and there are not a lot of ways to discover music that you could associate emotions with.

Problem: There is no real way to discover new music based on moods, activity or even location.

The solution: We created Resonate which helps users discover newly recommended songs based on moods, activity, and location preferences. 

Timeline: 8 weeks

The team: Meet Palan, Rob Nashed, Clay Tercek, Woo Song

My role: Product Design, Visual Design, User Experience Design and Research, and ReactJS development

 

 

UNDERSTANDING OUR USERS

Identifying who we are designing for

Whether young or old, everyone listens to music but we wanted to focus on people who use online music streaming services Spotify, Apple Music, Pandora, etc. Research showed that people between the age of 14 to 39 (mostly students and young professionals) listen to music online so we set our targeted audience to be students and young professionals.
